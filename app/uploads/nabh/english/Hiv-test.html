HIV TEST INFORMATION AND CONSENT

Indication for HIV Testing :
My doctor informed me that the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV) antigen test will be done on a sample of my blood. I understand that this test shows the presence or absence of the HIV (AIDS) virus. I understand that a negative test does not necessarily mean that I have not been infected with HIV. I have had a chance to ask questions about the meaning of these tests. I have been informed that I will be offered counseling from trained individuals if the results of the test show that I have, or that I might have, the HIV virus. I will also be told if my test is negative (shows nothing). If the test is unsure, another test may be suggested in the next six to twelve months.

Confidentiality :-
I understand that my test result will be kept confidential to the full extent required by law. I understand that particular care is being taken to maintain my records in a secure manner. There are five exceptions to confidentiality. The exceptions allow release to the local health authority as required, the ordering Doctor, health care personnel having a legitimate need to know, the subject or the person giving legal consent, and the spouse if the patient tests positive.

I understand that I can refuse consent and that testing will only be done without my consent for management of an accidental exposure of a health care worker to my blood or body fluids.

I do consent to the test for the HIV antibody / antigen.
